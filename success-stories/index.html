Representative Case Results

The following are representative of the types of cases we handle and outcomes we have achieved. Past results do not guarantee future outcomes, as every case depends on its unique facts and circumstances.

Asylum & Convention Against TortureAsylum Granted

Asylum & Relief Under the Convention Against Torture

The Challenge

We represent clients fleeing persecution due to political activities, religious beliefs, or membership in targeted social groups — including cases other attorneys have turned down.

The Outcome

Through comprehensive country condition evidence, expert testimony, and compelling personal declarations, we have successfully obtained asylum for clients facing removal, providing them with permanent protection in the United States.

Cancellation of RemovalRemoval Cancelled

Cancellation of Removal for Non-Permanent Residents

The Challenge

We defend long-term residents facing deportation who have U.S. citizen or permanent resident family members, demonstrating the exceptional and extremely unusual hardship removal would cause.

The Outcome

Through medical evidence, school records, expert testimony, and financial documentation, we have persuaded immigration judges to cancel removal and grant our clients lawful permanent residence.

O-1 Extraordinary Ability VisaO-1 Approved

O-1 Extraordinary Ability Visa

The Challenge

We prepare O-1 petitions for professionals with extraordinary ability or achievement, assembling the evidence needed to demonstrate sustained national or international acclaim.

The Outcome

Our petition packages include expert recommendation letters, evidence of awards and publications, media coverage, and documentation of significant contributions to the field.

P-3 Culturally Unique Artist VisaP-3 Approved

P-3 Culturally Unique Artist or Entertainer Visa

The Challenge

We represent artists and entertainers performing traditional or culturally unique art forms, preparing P-3 petitions that establish the cultural merit of their work.

The Outcome

Our petitions document the cultural uniqueness of the art form, the artist's credentials and international recognition, and the cultural value of planned U.S. performances.

U-Visa for Crime VictimsU-Visa Approved

U-Visa for Victims of Crime

The Challenge

We help victims of qualifying crimes who have cooperated with law enforcement obtain U-Visa protection, even when they face the prospect of deportation.

The Outcome

We work with law enforcement to obtain the required certification, prepare comprehensive declarations, and submit evidence of physical and psychological impact to secure lawful status, work authorization, and a path to permanent residence.
